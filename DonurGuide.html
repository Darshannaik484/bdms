Safety Guidelines for Blood Donors

For Donors:
  • You should be at least 17 years old (16 with permission in some places).
  • You need to weigh at least 110 pounds (50 kg).
  • You should be feeling healthy and not have any infections.
  • You can donate whole blood every 56 days, or eight weeks.
Before Donating:
  • Drink water: Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water before donating.
  • Eat a meal: Have a good meal before donating, especially one with iron (like spinach or meat).
  • Avoid alcohol and too much caffeine: These can dehydrate you or make you feel dizzy.
During the Donation:
  • Stay calm: It’s normal to feel a little nervous, but the staff will help you.
  • Let staff know if you feel unwell: If you feel lightheaded or uncomfortable, tell them right away.
  • The donation: They’ll take blood from your arm, which usually takes about 10-15 minutes.
After Donation:
  • Rest: Stay seated for a few minutes to recover.
  • Drink and snack: Drink water and have a snack to help you feel better.
  • Take it easy: Avoid heavy exercise for the rest of the day.
  • Watch for any issues: If you feel dizzy or see bruising, tell the staff.
