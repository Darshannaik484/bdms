Safety Guidelines for Blood Receivers

Blood Testing

  • Screening: All donated blood is carefully tested for infections (such as HIV, hepatitis, and other diseases) to ensure that it is safe to receive.

Compatibility Check

  • Blood type matching: The blood received is matched with the recipient’s blood type to prevent reactions.
  • Crossmatching: This is done to further ensure the blood is compatible with the recipient’s immune system.

Monitoring During the Transfusion

  • Observe for reactions: Medical staff will closely monitor the recipient for any signs of an allergic or adverse reaction to the transfusion, such as itching, fever, or difficulty breathing.
